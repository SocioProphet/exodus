# Product definition

Exodus is a migration-governance and evidence product, not just a collector.

It tracks:

- what provider surfaces exist,
- what data and workflow dependencies are attached to them,
- what has already been exported or replaced,
- what still blocks exit,
- what evidence supports those claims,
- and what sequence of actions yields the largest sovereignty gain.

## Primary users

- individual operators leaving closed ecosystems
- family-office or high-control users
- enterprise migration teams
- digital sovereignty / compliance teams
- platform operators guiding customers through staged exit plans

## Core jobs to be done

1. Inventory provider dependence.
2. Show a trustworthy picture of progress.
3. Quantify remaining lock-in.
4. Recommend the next best action.
5. Preserve evidence of what was collected, exported, and validated.
6. Produce decision-grade Phase 2 ingestion and archival plans before bulk movement.

## Product modes

- Inventory mode
- Exodus mode
- Forensic mode
- Continuous monitoring mode
